# Arogya Radar - Disease Profile Specs

## DISEASE: Tuberculosis

**A. CARDINAL SIGNS**
* Khaansi > 2 weeks (Persistent cough for more than 2 weeks)
* Raat ko pasina (Night sweats)
* Wazan kam hona (Unexplained weight loss)
* Khaansi mein khoon (Haemoptysis / Blood in sputum)

**B. DAY-BY-DAY PROGRESSION**
* **Day 0**: Mild fever, general fatigue ("Thakaan aur halka bukhar").
* **Day 5**: Fever persists, dry cough begins ("Bukhar nahi ja raha, sookhi khaansi shuru hui").
* **Day 9**: Cough worsens and becomes productive, noticeable weight loss, night sweats. **(Pivot Visit)**
* **Day 14**: Severe weakness, possible blood in sputum. Action required.

**C. DISCRIMINATING SYMPTOMS**
* "Raat ko pasina" (Night sweats)
* "Khaansi 2 hafte se zyada" (Cough > 2 weeks)
* "Wazan gir raha hai" (Weight loss)
* "Balgam mein khoon" (Blood in sputum)

**D. RED HERRINGS**
* *Fever and fatigue*: Shared with Dengue and Typhoid, but TB fever is usually low-grade and evening-peaking.
* *Body ache*: Common in all, but mostly Dengue.

**E. TYPICAL MISDIAGNOSIS**
* Frequently confused with Viral Fever or Bronchitis on Day 0 and Day 5, because the cough has not yet crossed the 2-week threshold and weight loss isn't apparent yet.

**F. CO-PRESENTATION RISK**
* TB can present with HIV, worsening the symptoms rapidly. In rural areas, it might also co-present with chronic respiratory issues like COPD, making the cough seem "normal" for the patient.

**G. ASHA ACTION THRESHOLD**
* Refer to PHC immediately if the cough lasts > 2 weeks AND night sweats/weight loss are present. Observe if it's just a cough for < 1 week.

## DISEASE: Dengue

**A. CARDINAL SIGNS**
* Achanak tez bukhar (Sudden high fever)
* Aankhon ke peeche dard (Retro-orbital pain)
* Jodo aur haddiyon mein dard (Severe joint/muscle pain / "breakbone")
* Lal daane (Skin rash)

**B. DAY-BY-DAY PROGRESSION**
* **Day 0**: Sudden onset of high fever, severe headache ("Achanak tez bukhar aur sar dard").
* **Day 5**: Fever might slightly drop but severe body ache and retro-orbital pain set in ("Aankhon ke peeche dard aur haddiyan toot rahi hain").
* **Day 9**: Skin rash appears, bleeding gums or extreme fatigue due to platelet drop. **(Pivot Visit)**
* **Day 14**: Recovery phase or progressed to Dengue Hemorrhagic Fever (shock).

**C. DISCRIMINATING SYMPTOMS**
* "Aankhon ke peeche dard" (Retro-orbital pain)
* "Jodo mein dard" (Joint pain)
* "Lal daane" (Rash)
* "Masoodon se khoon" (Bleeding gums)

**D. RED HERRINGS**
* *High fever and headache*: Can mimic Typhoid initially, but Typhoid fever is step-wise, whereas Dengue is sudden and extremely high.

**E. TYPICAL MISDIAGNOSIS**
* Confused with Chikungunya or standard Viral Fever on Day 0-3. Misdiagnosis happens because rashes don't appear until the fever subsides.

**F. CO-PRESENTATION RISK**
* Can co-present with Typhoid or Malaria. Co-presentation with Typhoid shows severe joint pain (Dengue) along with abdominal symptoms and diarrhea (Typhoid).

**G. ASHA ACTION THRESHOLD**
* Refer to PHC immediately if patient reports rashes, bleeding from gums/nose, or extreme dizziness (signs of platelet drop).

## DISEASE: Typhoid

**A. CARDINAL SIGNS**
* Bukhar badhta hi ja raha hai (Prolonged, step-wise fever)
* Pet dard aur kabz (Abdominal pain and initial constipation)
* Dast (Pea-soup diarrhea later in the progression)
* Gulabi daane (Rose spots - rare but specific)

**B. DAY-BY-DAY PROGRESSION**
* **Day 0**: Low grade fever, malaise, headache ("Halka bukhar aur ajeeb si thakaan").
* **Day 5**: Fever increases daily (step-wise), abdominal pain, constipation ("Bukhar roz badh raha hai, pet mein dard aur kabz hai").
* **Day 9**: High fever, diarrhea replaces constipation, severe weakness. **(Pivot Visit)**
* **Day 14**: Exhaustion, continuous high fever, risk of intestinal perforation.

**C. DISCRIMINATING SYMPTOMS**
* "Bukhar roz badh raha hai" (Step-wise increasing fever)
* "Pet dard ke saath dast" (Abdominal pain with diarrhea)
* "Kabz ke baad dast" (Constipation followed by diarrhea)

**D. RED HERRINGS**
* *Fever and headache*: Looks like Dengue or Malaria early on.
* *Fatigue*: Looks like TB or general viral infection.

**E. TYPICAL MISDIAGNOSIS**
* Often confused with food poisoning or Gastroenteritis around Day 5. The confusion occurs because the step-wise fever pattern is missed if temperature isn't charted.

**F. CO-PRESENTATION RISK**
* Can co-present with Dengue in endemic areas. The trajectory will show step-wise fever and stomach issues PLUS severe joint pain and rashes.

**G. ASHA ACTION THRESHOLD**
* Refer to PHC if high fever persists beyond Day 5 and is accompanied by severe abdominal pain or continuous diarrhea.
